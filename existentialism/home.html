Existentialism

Jean-Paul Sartre’s theory of Existentialism is characterized explicitly by the quote “existence precedes essence”. More precisely, it insists that because man’s physical form must have existed before his understanding of himself, he is therefore free to interpret and understand his existence in any way he sees fit. However, in deciding for himself what sort of man he is and would like to be, he also produces the image of the man that all men should be, and chooses for them as well. Despite the anxieties suffered by those who are aware of the immense responsibility in envisioning the ideal man for all of man, Sartre believed that we shouldn’t try to run or hide from this fact, and live on in full knowledge of it. We consider a video game to have existential themes if it encourages players to seek their own individualized sense of meaning or fun through a variety of different game systems and story elements.
