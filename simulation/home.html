Simulation

Simulation and Simulacra proposed by Jean Baudrillard are defined as follows. In an information-rich postmodern society, the rapid proliferation of digital content and mass production technologies results in the emergence of “copies without originals”. These objects, referred to as Simulacra, are not beholden to the reality they purportedly imitate and will eventually come to replace the “real” in “hyper-reality”. Baudrillard’s claims that such a state is characterized by individuals being unable to distinguish the real from the simulated . An example of this might be the seasonal pumpkin-spice drinks sold by Starbucks which don’t actually contain any pumpkin spice nor taste like pumpkins. We consider a video game to explore the themes of simulation and simulacra if it explicitly challenges or leverages the player’s awareness of their interaction with a simulated world to create a uniquely deep narrative or novel gameplay experience.
