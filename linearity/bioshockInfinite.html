Bioshock Infinite

Simulation and Reality

Summary

Bioshock Infinite takes place in the early 20th century in the floating city of Columbia. The astounding steampunk architecture and design of the city is juxtaposed with the city’s overwhelming culture of American exceptionalism and fervent devotion to its leader, Zachary Comstock.

The protagonist of the game is Booker DeWitt, who is assigned to retrieve a girl named Elizabeth. After a cold and chaotic introduction to one another, the two gradually get closer throughout the game, forming a sort of father-daughter relationship.

It is soon revealed that Elizabeth possesses immense powers, being capable of traveling through different dimensions that are ultimately formed by the decisions of the two. The multiple endings of the game encapsulate the overarching themes of fate and destiny and the paradoxical nature of making decisions that could shape your future or ultimately lead to a result one had attempted to avoid the entire time.

Analysis

The concept of alternate dimensions and the cyclical nature of preventing events that are unstoppable is similar to Jorge Luis Borge’s short story “The Library of Babel.” Aside from driving men to insanity and questioning the worth of a library which is practically inaccessible, the concept of an infinite library that contains both the answers to every possible question or theory in existence and also absolutely useless information ties well into the many dimensions that would be practically identical to each other, questioning the worth of these dimensions being created and what events would inspire little change.
