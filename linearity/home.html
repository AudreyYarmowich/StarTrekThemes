Non-Linearity in Narrative Story Telling

Non-Linearity in Narrative Story Telling

Postmodern works frequently challenge and subvert established conventions and styles of story-telling. We see this in Samuel Becket’s Molloy with how the first portion of the story is contained within just two paragraphs, and again with Italo Calvino’s If Upon a Winter’s Night a Traveler and its second person narrative. The unusual narrative forms implemented in both of these works demonstrate that writing is more flexible than the reader might come to expect and suggests future avenues of innovation. Furthermore, it provides a wholly unique and exclusive experience the reader might have never encountered before. We consider a video game to have unconventional stylings in both gameplay and story-telling if it deliberately subverts genre expectations and gameplay stereotypes to tell a story whose experiential impact could not have been otherwise achieved.
